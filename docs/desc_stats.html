Chapter 3 Human Milk Component Distributions

This chapter presents descriptive summaries of human milk composition across studies, visits, and randomized intervention arms. The purpose of these analyses is to characterize the empirical distributions of milk components prior to modeling intervention effects and to provide context for the magnitude, variability, and baseline heterogeneity of milk constituents across settings.

Interactive density plots and boxplots are provided for all measured milk components, including macronutrients, micronutrients, B-vitamins, human milk oligosaccharides, targeted metabolites, and other analytes defined in the IMiC outcome framework. Distributions are stratified by study, milk collection visit, and intervention arm, allowing visualization of between-site differences, within-site variability, and potential shifts in central tendency or dispersion associated with supplementation.

These figures are descriptive only. No covariate adjustment, hypothesis testing, or multiple-comparison correction is applied in this chapter. Apparent differences between arms or visits should therefore be interpreted as exploratory and may reflect baseline differences, sampling variability, or physiological variation across lactation, rather than causal intervention effects. Formal estimates of intervention effects are presented in subsequent chapters using prespecified statistical models.
